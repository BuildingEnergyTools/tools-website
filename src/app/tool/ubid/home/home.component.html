Does your organization have a "natural key" to unlock the power of its building-related data? Natural keys — like a VIN or Social Security number — are based on real-world attributes. Datasets with natural keys are much more powerful and require much less staff time to manage.

Addresses and lot numbers are "pseudo" keys. They can refer to more than one building and are prone to data entry errors. Datasets with pseudo keys are very hard to connect and difficult to manage.

"When you're working with datasets that are dependent on addresses, you realize what terrible identifiers they are."

— Steven Inclan, Miami-Dade County Information Technology Department

The Unique Building Identifier (UBID) is a natural key for buildings based on geospatial coordinates. Juridictions including Miami-Dade County, the District of Columbia and Portland, Maine have used UBIDs to unlock their data's potential and streamline program implementations.

With UBIDs, you can easily:

  • Implement benchmarking, audit and building performance standard policies.
  • Generate a truly unique ID for any building.
  • Simplify reporting for building owners.
  • Connect complementary datasets across your organization, unlocking the power of data.
  • Spend less time collecting, cleaning and managing building data.
  • Improve the quality and usefulness of data.
  • Consistently track a building's benchmarking and audit results over multiple reporting periods.
  • Leverage expert support from DOE and the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory.

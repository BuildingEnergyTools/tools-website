Would you like to see how a building stacks up against real-world peers? The Building Performance Database (BPD) features measured performance data for over 1 million U.S. buildings — the largest public collection of such information available.

"The BPD is a valuable source of information with powerful filters. Being able to filter by climate zone, building type, building construction year, and building square footage gives us key benchmarking data, even before we visit a facility."

— Maribella Ibarra, Energy Consultant at VEIC

With the BPD, you can easily:

  • View the real-world performance of existing buildings.
  • See the energy use, location and characteristics of over one million U.S. buildings.
  • Compare buildings to customized peer groups.
  • Filter data based on building type, location, size, age, occupancy and system characteristics.
  • Use data to set program efficiency targets.
  • Incorporate BPD data into your own tools, products or applications.
  • Leverage expert help from DOE and Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory.

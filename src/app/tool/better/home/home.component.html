The Building Efficiency Targeting Tool for Energy Retrofits (BETTER) is a free, award-winning web application available at https://better.lbl.gov/ that uses readily available building and monthly energy data to provide visibility into the operating energy performance of buildings and portfolios without site visits and complex modeling. The tool identifies immediate, cost-saving energy efficiency operational and technological measures and prioritizes further audits and studies.

How It Works

BETTER utilizes a novel, open-source analytical engine and user-friendly web interface to automatically analyze building and portfolio energy use in response to different weather conditions. With minimal data inputs, the tool benchmarks a building’s electric and fossil energy use against peers; quantifies energy, cost and greenhouse (GHG) reduction potentials at the building and portfolio levels; and recommends action-oriented energy efficiency measures to decarbonize and electrify buildings or portfolios, targeting specific savings levels. For more, visit https://better.lbl.gov/how_it_works/.

Who is Using BETTER?

BETTER is being used by U.S. state and local governments, energy service companies, multinational corporations, institutional investors, academia, and non-profit organizations. For more on pilot projects, visit better.lbl.gov/news/.

“The ability of the tool to go beyond benchmarking by offering energy efficiency measure recommendations is extremely valuable, particularly at times when on-site assessments are either impractical or not feasible.”

Eric Noller, Principal, Energy Resources Integration LLC, San Francisco, CA

Data Requirements

BETTER requires minimal data inputs:

  • Building ID or name
  • Location (U.S. zip code or city, state)
  • Gross floor area (excluding parking)
  • Primary building space type (i.e.,  50% of the gross floor area)
  • 12 consecutive months of energy use and cost for all fuels used by the building

Interoperability

BETTER can input data from ENERGY STAR® PortfolioManager® and a BuildingSync® file read/write capability is being added to facilitate data transfer between BETTER and Asset Score, Audit Template, and the Standard Energy Efficiency Data (SEED)TM Platform. SEED users will also have the option to access BETTER’s analytical reports for buildings from within the SEED Platform.
